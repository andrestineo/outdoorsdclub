The Best Scenic Trails for Fall Hiking

Autumn is a magical time to go hiking. As the leaves change and the air turns crisp, many trails transform into colorful landscapes perfect for exploring. If you're looking to make the most of fall hiking season, here are some of the best scenic trails across the country to experience the vibrant autumn foliage.

1. Blue Ridge Parkway, North Carolina & Virginia

The Blue Ridge Parkway is a must-visit for any fall hiking enthusiast. With over 469 miles of stunning vistas, it’s one of the most beautiful drives in America. Along the parkway, there are countless trails that offer breathtaking views of the Appalachian Mountains covered in brilliant fall colors.

2. Maroon Bells, Colorado

Located near Aspen, the Maroon Bells Scenic Loop is one of the most photographed spots in Colorado, and it's easy to see why. During the fall, the surrounding aspen trees turn a striking gold, creating a picturesque backdrop for a peaceful hike.

3. Acadia National Park, Maine

Acadia National Park is known for its rugged coastline and stunning fall foliage. The Jordan Pond Path is a great choice for hikers who want to enjoy the beauty of the park’s lakes and forests. In the fall, the red, orange, and yellow leaves provide a stunning contrast against the park's granite peaks.

4. Shenandoah National Park, Virginia

Shenandoah National Park’s Skyline Drive offers amazing views of the Shenandoah Valley, especially in the fall. The park has over 500 miles of trails to explore, with options for all skill levels. The Hawksbill Summit trail offers panoramic views of the valley below, framed by vibrant autumn foliage.

No matter where you choose to hike this fall, make sure to bring your camera to capture the beauty of the season. Fall hiking provides a unique opportunity to witness nature at its most colorful, so get out there and enjoy the trails before winter sets in!
